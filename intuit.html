Intuit Design Sprint

Home /Intuit Design Sprint

  • Goal: Redesign the TurboTax on-boarding experience to improve their user browsing experience
  • Date: November 18, 2018

  • Skills: User Research, Needfinding, Storyboarding, Prototyping

  • Tools: Figma, Adobe XD

  • Roles: UX & UI Designer, Researcher

Project Overview

The goal of this design sprint is to further improve and provide TurboTax on-boarding a confidence-building experience while also helping users to further understand TurboTax services.

The Problem

With the new service of TurboTax Live, their users need a reassuring and informative onboarding process as they often question the credibility of the CPAs and EAs that are providing their personal feedback and assistance for this service.

Brainstorming

After identifying the problem and establishing our mission statement, we start to brainstorm a possible solution by writing as many ideas we can in post-it notes then taping it into the wall before we narrow it to several important approaches.





Mission Statement(s)

We develop several approaches that can help to provide and ensure a smooth and reliable onboarding process for the TurboTax Live users. Each of these approaches communicates the context, problem, and proposed solution for the stakeholder in order to further narrow down our issues and concepts.

  • Ensure user and CPA/EA compatibility while accounting for implicit bias

  • Consider our users' mindset while trying to impart something useful to users

  • Build a relationship of trust between users and CPAs/EAs by empowering our user while considering practical constraints



The Solution

The mission statements then led to our proposed engagement model that addressed the root cause along with building confidence and empowers the users. The main features of this engagement model are adding area(s) of specialization and brief qualifications of the CPAs to give the users more information regarding the experts. Along with that feature, we also added a pop-up chat that can help users to further observe the reviews of their CPAs.



Lo-fi Prototyping

After finalizing our approach to the problem, we made a quick paper prototype that visualizes the significant and interactive elements we hope to achieve in our design.



Takeaways

As one of my first projects that were established out of conventional classroom settings, I find myself learning so much from this day-long event. One of the insights I find interesting is that the design process is very non-linear. Unlike what has always been taught to us during design classes, sometimes the design process itself involves a lot of back and forth between one stage to another to achieve a satisfying end-product. More importantly, I also learn that the documentation of a design workflow is also extremely important as it can further justify our decision when it comes to the design itself. Overall, I'm really proud and satisfied with our end-product as it showcases our knowledge and skill when it comes to user experience and interaction design.


Final Prototype

After refining our design, we then present our final design prototype in front of design experts to gain even more insights.

Future Directions

Since this is only a day-long design sprint, we also provided several steps that we need to take in order to finalize this design prototype. These directions include:

  • Conduct Usability Testing to gain more feedbacks from participants that resembles our target audience

  • Conduct A/B Testing to further determine which on-boarding design that provide users with better browsing experience.

  • Develop more engaging features with user experience in mind to further accommodate and assist them.

  • Repeat - because we believe that design process often involves a circle of Critique, Iterate, and Repeat.
