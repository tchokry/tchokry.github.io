Spark Track

Home /Spark Track

  • Goal: Build an interactive and innovative mobile information system to address users needs during home isolation due to COVID-19.
  • Date: March - June 2020

  • Skills: User Research, Needfinding, Storyboarding, Prototyping, Wireframing, User Testing

  • Tools: Figma, Adobe InDesign, Balsamic

  • Roles: UX & UI Designer, Researcher

Project Overview

The goal of this project is to conduct user research to understand problems and opportunities for design related to home isolation due to the COVID-19 pandemic along with building an interactive demo for a mobile information system desiged to address user needs.

Online Research

The first step of this project is to conduct an online secondary research in order to investigate different domestic settings to understand how individuals and families have adapted to home isolation affected by the COVID-19 pandemic. To get started, we explore online communities where the members can fully discuss their situation related to the topic of home isolation in which can help us to further comprehend this issue. Furthermore, we also conducted this research by investigating articles and journals that is related to living conditions affected by the COVID-19 outbreak. Some of our foundings are:

  • 36% of a manager’s time is spent in meetings and only 39% of those meetings achieve their objectives. These numbers are pre COVID-19, so we can only image how low that number are considering that everything is currently shifted online

  • 32 million workers are highly exposed to pandemic risk because their work require human interaction and are not easily converted for remote working

  • The majority of the social dynamic of these online communities that were build surrounding the topic of COVID-19 are mostly based on the idea of support groups that intended to provide a safe space for other people in sharing their feelings, thoughts, advice, or even coping mechanism when it comes to home isolation

  • The information presented in these communities changed and adapted during the progress of COVID-19, offering more insight into the current situation, changes in suggestions, and talking points came up as well

The Problem

After conducting the secondary online research, we agreed on the problem statement in which our users would like an outlet to help boost their productivity levels in order to build a stronger work ethic for themselves and with others while in home isolation.

Mission Statement

We aim to improve motivation and facilitate social interaction by creating a mobile app that encourages people stay productive together.

Moodboard

We chose several adjectives that capture the essence of the emotional experience of using Spark Track and presented it in a moodboard format. The main adjectives we chose are Calm and Focus.



Style Guide

Using the moodboard as a guide, we created a style guide with color such as red and blue to reflect the purpose of our application, which is calm and focus. We also chose typeface Poppins to keep the modern and simplistic theme of the application itself.







User Testing

Due to the circumstances, we are only able to conduct our user testing virtually online, mostly through Zoom. There's two stage to our user testing in which we first use our lo-fi prototype to gather some feedback before applying it to our hi-fi prototype and then conduct another user testing again to further improve the features of our application.

Lo-fi User Testing Insights:
  • Users were confused as to what the streaks and the level icons were

  • Consider having the “monthly” tab to pick specific days to track for the Add Habit features

  • Needs another way to illustrate user's progress so it's useful in terms of increaring incentive for continued use of the application

  • Users had a hard time understanding how this app differs from regular habit trackers or task management apps, but thought it was useful. There's a need to elaborate our intentions to create a platform where users can help each other achieve goals and develop habits

Hi-fi User Testing Insights:
  • First time users struggle to navigate through the app and suggest adding a tutorial at the beginning of the app

  • There's a need to create more incentive for users to use the app

  • Some users suggests that adding a tab where they can prioritize certain tasks can be beneficial for the app



Takeaways

This project helps me to explore basic practicies of interaction design through a focus on physical, visual, and digital prototyping. Since it follows human-centered design process in which includes online research, concept generation, prototyping, user testing, and further improvement, I have learn to work effectively whether as an individual or as a part of a team to create an interactive and informative mobile information system. Furthermore, since the topic of this project is surrounding the COVID-19 pandemic - especially home isolation, it has given me an insight to remote design work in which further helps me in terms of future remote job opportunities.
